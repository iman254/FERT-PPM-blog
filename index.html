What is FERT PPM?

FERT PPM is a web application me and my team of two software Engineers designed to bring uniformity in data distribution in the world of fertigation usage. It is an application that enables regulation of fertilizer being injected in plants to ensure correct amounts of nutrients are being utilized hence promoting quality growth of the crop. Since the fertilizers are delivered in a dissolved form, the distribution of the nutrients in the soil and other growing media is almost the same as the distribution of water. The app incorporates instances of soil and hydroponic use. A careful consideration of nutrient concentrations in fertigation programs is also required to contain nutrient levels within acceptable contaminant limits.

What is Fertigation?

Fertigation is a term that combines two words: "fertilization" and "irrigation." It refers to the process of applying fertilizers and nutrients to plants through an irrigation system. Instead of applying fertilizers separately from irrigation water, fertigation allows for the simultaneous delivery of water and nutrients to crops. Fertigation System In a fertigation system, a liquid fertilizer solution or soluble fertilizers are mixed with water and distributed through an irrigation system, such as drip irrigation or sprinklers. This allows for precise and controlled application of nutrients directly to the plant's root zone. A lot of the fertigation systems currently in use, have fertilizer injectors that facilitate the distribution of fertilizers. While creating FERT PPM we assumed the injector system that would be used would be in relation to the units assimilated in the app. Fertigation offers several advantages over traditional methods of fertilization. It ensures a more efficient use of fertilizers since the nutrients are delivered directly to the plants' roots, reducing losses due to runoff or leaching. It also enables the farmer to adjust the nutrient levels according to the specific needs of different crops or growth stages, leading to optimal plant nutrition. Furthermore, fertigation allows for better nutrient uptake by plants since the application occurs during irrigation when the root system is more receptive. It provides a more uniform distribution of nutrients throughout the field, which can result in improved crop yield, quality, and consistency. Overall, fertigation is a technique that combines irrigation and fertilization, offering precision, efficiency, and flexibility in delivering nutrients to crops, making it a popular choice in modern agriculture practices.

Technical challenges

To start with, we view our system as a full-stack web application. That said, it incorporates up to 5 front and backend languages and platforms, e.g. JavaScript, Python and Django, PHP, HTML, CSS among others. This makes it challenging to manage dependencies. Among the many bugs we encountered, the one that stood out for us was where we had to migrate the project to the deployment environment. The issue comes in getting the domain name to run in HTTPS protocol and SSH certificate. Considering that the project is big, the amount of time it took for deployment to be initialized was extensive. The challenges associated with managing project dependencies, implementing dynamic sorting and filtering, and handling file uploads were successfully overcome with a thorough study and adjustment of the project plan. The team remains vigilant and adapts to handle additional unforeseen complications.
